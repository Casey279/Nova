Damages from Sullivan.

William F. Case yesterday filed
amended complaints in the office of the
clerk of the superior court against M. C.
Sullivan. Case alleges that he was a
mechanic and doing well on the 27th
day of July, 1891, when without any
provocation or cause he was assaulted
and seriously pounded by Mr. Sullivan,
He says he was pounded about the head
and breast and his leg broken and that
he was otherwise injured. He was com-
pelled to get the services of a surgeon,
which cost him $100, and lost $200 worth
ol time, He also suffered $15,000 worth
ot agony which he demands payment
for, and therefore asks $15,300 and the
costs of action.
